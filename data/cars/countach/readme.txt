Lamborghini Countach LP400 (1974)

12 Cylinder in V (60°), DOHC, 6 Carbs Weber 45DCOE 96/97, 2 barrel, 3.9-liter engine.
375 bhp, 315 km/h

Introduced as a prototype in 1971, the Countach gave the sport car a new shape and meaning. The wedge-shaped coupe made in Santagata Bolognese was the very first car to overpass the barrier of 300 km/h and remained the world fastest car since the debut of the Ferrari F40.
